## Niche 1: Câu cá / Fishing

The Vietnamese fishing content scene on Facebook splits into roughly four buckets:
1. **Miền Tây freshwater creators** — cá lóc, cá tra, cá trê in mương / ruộng / sông Mê Kông. Highly viral, "bắt cá khủng" miracle catches.
2. **Lure / câu lure / câu jig** — younger, urban anglers; gear-heavy; bass-style aesthetics imported from JP/US.
3. **Câu đài / câu cần tay (pole fishing)** — older demographic, hồ dịch vụ content, technique-focused.
4. **Sea / câu biển / câu đêm offshore** — boat trips, big tuna / cá bớp, dramatic weather footage.

Most viral Facebook traffic comes from buckets 1 and 4 — Page reup operators chop YouTube videos from creators below into 30-90s reels.

### Patterns to copy
- **Two-handed hero-catch cover frame** — every viral reup uses the same composition: angler centered, fish held up at chest height, sky behind. Train your editor on this.
- **First 1.5 seconds = the strike**. Don't show walking, baiting, waiting. Cold-open on the rod bending or the splash. Tease backstory inside the clip, not before it.
- **"Cả xóm kéo ra xem"** social-proof B-roll — neighbors crowding around the catch. Pages without crowd shots underperform by ~30%+ in informal observation.
- **Sound design**: keep ambient cicadas/water + a single exclamation ("Trời ơi nó bự quá!"). Skip music beds — they read as fake.
- **Repeat-character episodic universe** — viewers come back for the 2-Lúa-style sidekick, not the fish. Cast a recurring "ông chú" character early.
- **2-4 reels/day from the same Page** to ride FB's freshness signal; rotate hooks (catch reveal / technique / blooper / cook-the-catch) to avoid same-style fatigue.
- **Cook-the-catch outro** — extending a fishing reel into a 10-second cooking shot dramatically improves watch-time + saves.

### Differentiation wedge
- **AVOID — saturated angles:** generic "cá khủng miền Tây" miracle catches (50+ pages doing this daily, all reupping the same 3 creators); staged "bắt bằng tay" tank-fish content (audience is wising up); pure unboxing/gear reviews in Vietnamese (low CTR on FB feed).
- **OWN — fresh angles:**
  1. **Urban Hanoi / Saigon canal micro-fishing** — Hồ Tây, kênh Nhiêu Lộc, công viên ponds. Almost nobody covers it with cinematic quality; it taps the 20-something city audience that fishing reup pages currently miss.
  2. **"Trả cá về sông" catch-and-release storytelling** — moral / wholesome angle is underused; rides on growing ecological awareness; differentiates from kill-and-cook majority.
  3. **Female angler POV** — Vietnamese fishing FB is ~95% male presenters. A confident female lead (not bikini-bait — actual skilled angler) is a wide-open lane with built-in algorithmic novelty.

## Niche 2: Sinh tồn / Bushcraft / Survival

The VN survival niche is smaller and more YouTube-native than fishing, but FB reups are growing fast in 2024-2026. Audiences split into:
1. **Primitive-building** — Primitive Technology clones (clay ovens, bamboo huts, no-talking).
2. **Bushcraft / outdoor-skills** — knife, fire, tarp shelter, foraging; mid-length tutorials.
3. **"Sinh tồn trong rừng X ngày"** challenge format — solo creator + camera, 24h/72h/7-day stays.
4. **Ethnic-minority lifestyle vlogs adjacent to bushcraft** — H'Mông, Tày, Thái creators showing daily mountain life; often miscategorized as "survival" by FB pages.

### Patterns to copy
- **Countdown / timer overlay** — "Ngày 1 / Ngày 2 / Ngày 3" or "Còn 12h nước" boosts retention by giving viewers a progress bar.
- **Open with a problem, not a setting** — "Hết nước, trời sắp tối" beats "Hôm nay mình vào rừng…". Start mid-crisis.
- **Single tool constraint** — "Chỉ với 1 con dao", "Không mang bật lửa" reframes mundane footage as a challenge. Algorithm gold.
- **Tactile close-ups** — fire-starting sparks, knife shaving wood, mud being shaped. Slow it down, mic it loud.
- **Quiet creator persona** — successful VN survival pages talk *less* than fishing pages. Let ambient sound + on-screen text do the work; subtitles for non-headphone scrollers.
- **Build-with-payoff structure** — show the finished shelter / oven / trap in the first 2 seconds, then rewind to "Đây là cách mình làm". Reverse-chronology hooks dominate this niche.
- **Cross-post to vùng-cao audience** by featuring ethnic-minority gear/food authentically — but only if you can do it without cosplay (audience smells fakes).

### Differentiation wedge
- **AVOID — saturated angles:** Primitive-Technology-style no-talking clay-building (market is full and the original-creator bar is high); generic "10 kỹ năng sinh tồn" listicles (commodity content with low watch-time); fake "lạc trong rừng" staged drama (audience trust is collapsing on these).
- **OWN — fresh angles:**
  1. **Urban survival / blackout kit / flood prep for Vietnam-specific scenarios** — typhoon season, miền Trung flooding, Saigon blackout drills. Practical, timely, ad-friendly, and almost nobody is doing it in Vietnamese.
  2. **"Sinh tồn 1 đêm trong thành phố"** — homeless-perspective survival in HCM/HN, sleeping rough, sourcing food legally. Edgy but viral if done with dignity, not exploitation.
  3. **Mother-daughter or father-son bushcraft duo** — current niche is ~100% solo male creators. A family/teaching dynamic opens a totally new emotional register and unlocks female viewers + brand-safety for sponsors.

## Cross-niche playbook

These tactics generalize across both fishing and survival and should inform the Shortform Factory pipeline regardless of niche:

- **Reel length sweet spot: 22-45 seconds.** Under 20s caps watch-time; over 60s loses scrollers. Build all templates to this band.
- **Vertical 9:16, safe-zone subtitles** — FB still crops top/bottom for UI. Keep all text inside the middle 80%.
- **Cover frame ≠ first frame.** Pages that set a custom cover (the "money shot" frame, not the cold-open frame) outperform on FB feed where the cover is what stops the scroll.
- **Two captions strategy** — one short hook on-video (large font, 3-7 words), one longer Page-post caption with hashtags and a CTA. Don't conflate them.
- **Hashtag mix: 3 broad + 3 niche + 1 trend.** E.g., `#câucá #vietnam #giảitrí` + `#cálócđồng #câulure #miềntây` + whatever's trending that week.
- **Comment-pinning** as a CTA channel — pin a comment with a question ("Bạn từng câu được cá to nhất bao nhiêu kg?") to seed engagement; FB rewards the resulting reply volume.
- **Cross-post to a Reels-only sister Page** for the same content with different covers — doubles inventory at near-zero cost.
- **Don't run paid boost in week 1** of a Page — let organic shape the algorithm's understanding of your audience; boost only proven winners after ≥10 reels.
- **Repurpose, don't repost** — every long-form should yield 3-5 reels with distinct hooks. The reup pages we compete with already do this; we must do it better and faster.

## Hook & caption pattern library — Top 10 VN Facebook reel hooks

For each pattern: why it works + a sample completion for **Fishing (F)** and **Survival (S)**.

1. **"Không ai nghĩ anh này sống được khi…"**
   *Why it works:* curiosity gap + implicit stakes (survival outcome unknown) forces the viewer to stay for resolution.
   F: "Không ai nghĩ anh này kéo nổi con cá ấy lên bờ…"
   S: "Không ai nghĩ anh này sống được sau 3 ngày trong rừng không nước…"

2. **"Cả làng phải kinh ngạc khi thấy…"**
   *Why it works:* social proof — if a whole village reacts, it must be worth watching. Triggers FOMO.
   F: "Cả làng phải kinh ngạc khi thấy con cá lóc khổng lồ này…"
   S: "Cả bản phải kinh ngạc khi thấy căn nhà chàng trai dựng trong 7 ngày…"

3. **"Thử ngay 1 lần để không hối hận"**
   *Why it works:* prescription + loss-aversion framing ("hối hận"). Converts viewers into doers — high save rate.
   F: "Thử ngay mẹo móc mồi này 1 lần để không hối hận khi đi câu…"
   S: "Thử ngay cách nhóm lửa không cần bật lửa 1 lần để không hối hận…"

4. **"Bí mật ít người biết về…"**
   *Why it works:* exclusivity + curiosity gap. Frames the viewer as an insider for watching.
   F: "Bí mật ít người biết về cách câu cá lóc mùa nước nổi…"
   S: "Bí mật ít người biết về việc tìm nước sạch trong rừng…"

5. **"Chỉ với X và Y, bạn có thể…"**
   *Why it works:* constraint hook — extreme economy of means promises a magic trick.
   F: "Chỉ với 1 sợi dây và 1 lưỡi câu, anh ấy bắt được cả thúng cá…"
   S: "Chỉ với 1 con dao và 1 cây tre, dựng được căn lều qua đêm…"

6. **"Ai ngờ dưới [nơi quen thuộc] lại có…"**
   *Why it works:* defamiliarizes the everyday — turns a mương / ao / khu rừng near home into a treasure site.
   F: "Ai ngờ dưới con mương sau nhà lại có đàn cá khủng đến vậy…"
   S: "Ai ngờ dưới gốc cây trong rừng lại có nguồn thức ăn không tưởng…"

7. **"Cái kết khiến ai cũng bất ngờ"**
   *Why it works:* explicit cliffhanger; the line is so overused it's become a category cue ("this is an entertaining reel"). Works *because* it's a genre signal, not despite it.
   F: "Đi câu lúc nửa đêm và cái kết khiến ai cũng bất ngờ…"
   S: "Một mình ngủ trong hang đá và cái kết khiến ai cũng bất ngờ…"

8. **"Đừng làm điều này khi… [hoạt động]"**
   *Why it works:* negative framing + safety stakes. Educational reels with this hook over-index on shares because viewers tag friends.
   F: "Đừng làm điều này khi đi câu cá vào ban đêm nếu không muốn về tay không…"
   S: "Đừng làm điều này khi gặp rắn trong rừng…"

9. **"Sau X ngày / X giờ, kết quả là…"**
   *Why it works:* time-investment proof — the creator's sunk cost becomes the viewer's reason to watch the payoff.
   F: "Sau 48 giờ không ngủ ngoài sông, kết quả là…"
   S: "Sau 7 ngày sống một mình trong rừng, kết quả là…"

10. **"Tưởng đùa nhưng có thật — [hành động bất thường]"**
    *Why it works:* explicit truth-claim against an unbelievable visual. Pre-empts the "fake" objection and dares the viewer to verify.
    F: "Tưởng đùa nhưng có thật — bắt cá bằng tay không trong 5 phút…"
    S: "Tưởng đùa nhưng có thật — đun nước sôi chỉ bằng đá và lửa…"
